The rollout of this IT solution would need a phase-based implementation strategy to ensure that the company’s main services are not disrupted whilst changes are being implemented, whilst also not slowing down workflow and requiring staff to adjust to this new system, taking up valuable time. There will be priority which would be given to essential systems such as the Square EPOS system, inventory management software, and the network system, as these support the core services such as sales, stock control, and online services whilst also amounting to the least amount of change to the companies current system and instead being an upgrade. By implementing these first, the business can continue working as usual while other services, such as staff collaboration tools and backup systems, are introduced slowly and staff training is given as necessary. This solution applies to both storefront and online-based service delivery. The integration that occurs between the EPOS system, Square inventory management, Shopify, and Amazon FBA makes sure that stock levels are updated automatically across all services, lowering extra manual workload by automating it and removing the possibility of human error. This significantly reduces the risks possible consisting of overselling, pricing errors and overstocking and ensures customers are told accurate info about product availability. While there is pricing and setup time that needs to be addressed with implementing these, this is justified by the improvement in efficiency, reduced manual workload, and increased reliability of service delivery, thus increasing revenue income and paying back the amount spent in costs. But despite this, the cloud services require an internet connection to be used, which could end up with a lot of data unable to be accessed when a connection isn't available. This risk is mitigated through the inclusion of a 5G backup connection and local network infrastructure, ensuring that services can continue or be quickly restored in the event of an outage. 2.
